Shaping your gathering

Prepare
Your Event

October 17 · 2026

Ancestral Wisdom  ·  Regenerative Action  ·  Your Community

Your event. Your community. Your culture.

Origin regional events are locally created — which means there is no single template to follow. A ceremony on a beach, a concert in a park, a school activation, a meditation circle, a large festival stage — all of it belongs. What matters is that your gathering carries intention, welcomes all, and honours the Earth we share.

Shaping your programme

  • Choose a meaningful venue — a park, beach, concert hall, community centre, sacred site, or open land.
  • Weave in local cultural traditions — music, storytelling, ceremony, dance, prayer, or art native to your place.
  • Include a regenerative action — seed planting, tree planting, community clean-up, food garden project, or any hands-in-earth moment.
  • If including Indigenous ceremony, ensure proper permissions, acknowledgement, respect, and fair compensation are in place before the event.
  • Make it accessible — free entry options where possible, wheelchair access, child-friendly elements.

Zero-waste planning

Origin events are asked to aim for zero single-use plastics and leave-no-trace practices. This is not a rule — it is an invitation to model the regenerative future we are gathering to celebrate.

If your event generates profit, you are asked to donate to a local nonprofit — ideally one aligned with regeneration, Indigenous wisdom, ecology, youth, or ecological restoration. After the event, you'll be invited to share which organisation you supported and the donation amount.
